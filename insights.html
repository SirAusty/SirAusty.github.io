Phase 1

Unlearning the Retail Trap

If you trade like the 90% who lose, you become the liquidity (the fuel) for the banks. Let's unlearn the bad habits.

1. Liquidity vs. Support & Resistance

Retail traders are taught to buy at "Support" lines. Institutions see those lines as clusters of stop-losses ready to be hunted. This is called Liquidity.

2. Candlestick Reality

Candles aren't just shapes or patterns. They represent real buying and selling pressure. A long wick isn't just a "pin bar," it's a visual rejection of price.

Phase 2

The Smart Money Edge (SMC)

Now we learn how the algorithm actually delivers price. You will learn the exact terminology professionals use to track the market.

Market Structure (BOS & CHoCH)

A Break of Structure (BOS) means the trend is continuing safely. A Change of Character (CHoCH) is your first warning sign that the trend is reversing.

Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

When the market moves too fast, it leaves an empty space called an Imbalance or FVG. Price almost always acts like a magnet, coming back to fill this gap before continuing.

The Judas Swing

A fake move engineered right when a major trading session opens (like London or New York). It tricks early traders into the wrong direction before the real move happens.

Phase 3

Risk & Execution Setup

A good strategy without risk management will still blow your account. This is how you protect your capital like a casino protects its vault.

Risk of Ruin

If you risk 10% per trade, you only need 10 losses to lose everything. If you risk 1%, you need 100 losses in a row. Math is your best defense.
