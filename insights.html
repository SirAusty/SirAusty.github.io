Module 1: Smart Money Concepts (SMC)

An Order Block (OB) represents the last opposite candle before an institutional displacement of price that breaks structural points (BOS/CHoCH).

  • Bullish OB: Lowest down-close candle prior to an upward break.
  • Bearish OB: Highest up-close candle prior to a downward break.

We target the open or 50% equilibrium level of the block for precision entries, keeping invalidation points tight directly beyond the block's tail.

A Fair Value Gap (FVG) is a 3-candle structural imbalance. It exists when the wick of Candle 1 and the wick of Candle 3 do not overlap, leaving an imbalance in Candle 2.

Algorithms return to these gaps to deliver fair pricing. Stacking entries inside an FVG correlated with discount/premium zones offers exceptional risk-to-reward parameters.

Boom indices spike instantly upward; Crash indices drop instantly downward. These movements happen within a single tick, creating a structural anomaly unique to synthetics.

⚠️ CRITICAL RISK WARNING:

Standard Stop-Loss orders **do not trigger** inside an active spike. If a Crash index drops violently past your stop loss, the algorithm bypasses your order and executes your close at the absolute bottom tip of the spike. Always adjust your risk percentage lower on Boom/Crash to account for potential slippage.
